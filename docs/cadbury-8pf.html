Synthesis · Final Verdict
The Creme Egg is a Hybrid that was breached twice — and neither event was ever declared.
At tf0 (1963) the egg had a Fry's milk chocolate shell. Around 1971, that shell was silently replaced with Cadbury Dairy Milk — an undeclared formula change that should have been recorded as a Hybrid with a breach badge. It was not. The egg that became culturally iconic from 1985 onward was not the tf0 egg: it was the undeclared Hybrid. In 2015, Mondelez removed Dairy Milk — the second undeclared shell formula change in the egg's history. Their defence that Dairy Milk was never formally part of the product's declared identity is technically true of tf0, but fatally undermined by the fact that their own predecessors silently installed it without declaration. You cannot disclaim what you silently established. The 2015 breach is evaluated against the 1985-consolidated Hybrid-state Descriptor, and it fails on that basis — plus the A6 concealment violation. The lineage endures as a Hybrid, scarred twice, with both scars absent from the institutional record. Consumer memory carries what the institution refuses to write down.
